# Economic Development Platform

## Small Business Support
- Establish a Frederick Small Business Innovation Fund — $2M annually from county revenue
- Streamline business licensing from 45 days to 15 days through digital-first processing
- Create a "Buy Frederick First" campaign with local procurement preferences for county contracts
- Partner with Frederick Community College for workforce training in trades and technology

## Tax Policy
- Oppose property tax increases that disproportionately burden small businesses
- Support tax credits for businesses that hire locally and pay living wages ($18+/hour)
- Push for a commercial vacancy tax on downtown properties left empty over 12 months

## Downtown Revitalization
- Invest in mixed-use development along East Patrick Street corridor
- Expand free parking hours downtown to support retail foot traffic
- Support the Creative District designation for arts-based economic development

## Key Statistics (verified)
- Frederick County small businesses employ 62% of the private workforce
- Average time to open a business in Frederick: 45 days (state avg: 30 days)
- Downtown vacancy rate: 12% (2025 data)
