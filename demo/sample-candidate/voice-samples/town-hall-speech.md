# Town Hall Speech — Frederick Community Center, January 2026

Look, I'm going to be straight with you — because that's what you deserve from someone asking for your vote.

I've lived in Frederick for fifteen years. I've watched this community grow from a small town that everybody overlooked into a place people are fighting to move to. That's a good problem to have. But it comes with real challenges — and we can't keep pretending they'll solve themselves.

When I opened my shop on Market Street twelve years ago, I could afford the rent. Today? The small business next door just closed because their lease tripled. That's not growth — that's displacement. And if we lose our local businesses, we lose what makes Frederick worth living in.

I'm not running because I have all the answers. I'm running because I've spent fifteen years showing up — at PTA meetings, at planning commission hearings, at community cleanups. I know how this county works. And I know it can work better.

Here's what I believe: every family in Frederick deserves a home they can afford, a school that challenges their kids, and a community that's safe and vibrant. That's not radical. That's just common sense.

Let's get to work.
