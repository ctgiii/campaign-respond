# Op-Ed — Frederick News-Post, February 2026
## "Frederick's Future Depends on Who We Include"

Frederick County stands at a crossroads. We can continue growing as a bedroom community for DC commuters, or we can build something better — a self-sustaining economy where people live, work, and thrive right here.

I hear from business owners every week. They tell me the same thing: they want to stay in Frederick, but the county isn't making it easy. Licensing takes too long. Rents are climbing. Workforce training doesn't match what employers need.

These aren't partisan problems. The small business owner on South Market and the teacher at Oakdale Elementary and the nurse at Frederick Health all want the same thing — a county government that works as hard as they do.

My approach is simple: invest in people, cut red tape, and make decisions based on data, not ideology. We don't need more politicians who talk about "economic development" while handing tax breaks to out-of-state corporations. We need leaders who support the businesses already here — the ones that sponsor Little League teams and hire our neighbors.

Frederick's best days aren't behind us. But they won't happen by accident. They'll happen because we chose to invest in each other.
